Experience and Education



  • Propulsion Development Engineer at Airbus Defence and Space

    2012-now


    Currently, I work at Airbus Defence & Space in Bremen where I am a Development Engineer for Liquid Propulsion Upper Stages. My main tasks as a member of the production team are to support the Ariane 5 production with technical and data analyses. Furthermore, I am involved in software development and in studies of future launchers with the following activities:

    - Planning and development of a software for storing, analysing and visualising flight data. The software is written in MATLAB and a corresponding web application is in development.

    - I am Scrum Product Owner for a software that will be automating the design of liquid propulsion upper stages involving the engineering disciplines propulsion, thermal, mission analysis and design / structure.

    - Responsible for analyses and sizing of propulsion systems for future launchers.


    Bremen, Germany

  • M. Sc. Systems Engineering

    2013-2015


    From 2013 to 2015 I studied Systems Engineering at the University of Bremen while working half time at Airbus. The specialisation of the studies was Space Systems Engineering and the Systems Engineering studies themselves are split equally into three fields:

    - Mechanical Engineering

    - Electrical Engineering

    - Computer Science

    During the studies I was able to participate in many group projects and learned programming in Java. The main team project of the studies was to build a test stand for the drive train of a wind turbine. This involved conception of the project, setting up the test bench with sensors, electrical wiring, programming the PLC (Programmable Logic Controller) and programming a visualisation and analysis software in LabVIEW.

    My master thesis was on the Development of a "Hydrogen Peroxide Propulsion Test Bench". Here, I designed a test bench for a hydrogen peroxide rocket engine. The project involved design and sizing of the test bench, selection and ordering of all components and planning all required safety measures, integration procedures and operating procedures. Furthermore, I tested material compatibility to hydrogen peroxide of all materials present in test bench components.


    University of Bremen, Germany

  • Dual Study Program at Astrium

    2008-2012


    From 2008 to 2012 I completed a dual study program at the company Astrium GmbH in Bremen in cooperation with the Universtiy of Applied Sciences Bremen. At Astrium, I finished an apprenticeship and I am now a certified mechatronics, while I studied a bachelor of engineering degree at the university. During my time at Astrium I was able to get an insight into different departments and participated in various team building trainings.

    I wrote my bachelor thesis on the "HPGP Thruster Performance Model Definition for an RCS of a Launcher Upper Stage". The main part of the thesis was to create a simplified model of a rocket engine working with "Green Propellants" in the software EcosimPro.


    Bremen, Germany
